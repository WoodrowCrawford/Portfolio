Project Overview

The Wild Wild East is a wacky western visual novel adventure made by a team for a game jam. I was one of the programmers, and the inventory system was my main job on it. It's a chaotic, funny world full of odd characters to meet and shoot the breeze with, living and not.

What I worked on

My main job on this one was the inventory system, the whole thing from picking items up to using them later. Items get grabbed when the player walks into them and drop into the next open slot, and clicking a slot uses the item and clears it out. I set the items up as their own reusable assets so the team could add new ones without touching code, and built the inventory itself as a slide-out panel that animates open and tucks away during conversations. This was the project where Unity's UI really clicked for me, since almost the whole thing lives in it.

Development Insights

This one was a fun, chaotic project to be part of. The team leaned all the way into the absurd western theme, and getting to build alongside artists and writers going for that kind of humor made it a nice change from my solo work. It was also my first time working with a team spread across different time zones, so I learned a lot about how much clear communication matters when you can't just turn and ask someone a question.
